# Age 1

Special Occasion: Hunning Taag. The Budding Moon. Considered the new year/ new cycle start. happens on the first new moons of the spring once the hornwood trees begin to bud. represents survival of the last winter. Observers will stay up to see the first sliver of the moon and toast with that years ice wine. A sheep is usually slaughtered for every 25 or so people and most people feast well into the night. The day leading up to the feast is usually a ritualistic fast

Good Luck: Ker Utur. Heavy Branches. When forriging the ultimate show of luck is finding a bush yet to be discovered by the other denizins of the forest. laden with berries, the brances bow with small victories aginst hunger.

fruit are seen as objects of good luck and fortune. 

Death: Manhk. Death. There is no euphimism for death or an instinct to avoid speaking of it. As much a part of life as eating and sleeping. It is not that the loss of a love one is not sad, greif is still a part of death. but with the concentration of hunters and the harsh nature of the mountain. Death must be respected and faced with the honor it deserves.

Create a word: Nokal. Cruel. The same root as very cold. The winter is not considered evil but it can be cruel and harsh. its disposition for its unforgivableness gave rise to a term for cruelness.

Create a word related: Nokahi. Somthing that is very cold

Resource: Brode, BahHuung Ger. Bronze, the low peoples scale. Metal is scarce in the mountain vilage. fortunatly what trade does comes to the village brings with it a stead supply of bronze. Thraid and Roch are villages traders they take their cart down the mountain twice a year to trade with the lowlands. bronze is used for nails, weapons, tools. Some beasts have bronze like scales that shed them in the fall, thus the name.

Expletive: Thish, Crap. An onomatapia of excrament hitting the leaves of the forrest floor. A newer term and one that older people would find crass.

# Age 2

Traitor: Jeduldur. from the term, Ker Okasha er yet Huld. which is to say that someone has a full belly and empty hands.
Thoes accused of and found guilty by the presideing body are often given the punishment of shuned survival.

Shuned Survival: Gott nog. The starving year. starting on Hunning Taag a family undergoing the Gott nog is ignored by the village. They must do everything alone. No villager is allowed to acknowlage them or help them. They may not use any resource of the village including its reserves of food. Winter is particualarly difficult. Many starve to death. Thoes who make it through the winter to the next Hunning Taag are welcomed back into the Village.
